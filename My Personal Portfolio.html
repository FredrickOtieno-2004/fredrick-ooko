Ugali Fish

Ingredients: For the Ugali: 2 cups maize flour (cornmeal) 4 cups water A pinch of salt For the Fish: 4-6 pieces of your preferred fish (tilapia, tilapia fillets, or any other fish you like) 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or cooking oil of your choice) 1 onion, chopped 2 tomatoes, chopped 2-3 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon ginger, grated 1 green bell pepper, chopped (optional) 1-2 tablespoons tomato paste (optional for richer flavor) 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon paprika Salt and pepper to taste Fresh coriander or parsley for garnish A squeeze of lemon or lime (optional) Instructions: For the Ugali: Boil water: In a large saucepan, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add a pinch of salt to the water for flavor. Add maize flour: Slowly pour in the maize flour while stirring constantly with a wooden spoon to avoid lumps. Cook: Continue stirring as the mixture thickens. After a few minutes, the ugali will start pulling away from the sides of the pot. Continue stirring for about 5-7 minutes until it is firm and smooth. Serve: Once it's cooked, remove from the heat and let it sit for a minute or two. Shape it into a round or oval shape using a spoon or your hands. For the Fish: Prepare the fish: Clean the fish, removing the scales and guts. If using whole fish, you can fry them whole or cut them into pieces. Cook the fish: Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium heat. Season the fish with salt, pepper, and any other preferred spices. Fry the fish: Cook the fish in the hot oil until golden and crispy on both sides. Remove from the pan and set aside. (Alternatively, you can bake the fish for a lighter version.) Prepare the sauce: In the same pan, add more oil if necessary, and saut√© the onions, garlic, and ginger until softened. Add tomatoes: Add the chopped tomatoes and cook for about 5 minutes until they soften and break down into a sauce. Add spices: Stir in the tomato paste (if using), paprika, ground coriander, cumin, and any additional spices you like. Cook for 2-3 more minutes. Add fish back to the sauce: Add the fried fish back to the pan and gently toss to coat in the tomato sauce. Let it simmer for 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Optional garnish: Sprinkle with chopped fresh coriander or parsley for an extra fresh flavor and squeeze some lemon or lime juice on top. Serving: Serve the fish and sauce over the ugali for a hearty meal. Ugali can be used to scoop up the fish and sauce. Enjoy with your favorite vegetables or a side salad!
